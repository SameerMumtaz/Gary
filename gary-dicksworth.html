📜 The Dossier of Gary Dicksworth III
Born: Zar'veth, 4th Astral Warband. Trained from birth to hunt Mind Flayers. Exceptional warrior. Catastrophically charming.

The Incident: Over fourteen years, Zar'veth accumulated entanglements across multiple planes — three human women, two elves, a dwarf cleric (who has since involved her deity), a sea hag (he does not discuss this), a tiefling merchant, a half-orc paladin, and — in what his commanding officer described as "a diplomatic incident of staggering stupidity" — Xal'ixx the Molten, Red Dragon. Offspring: eight to eleven. Gary has stopped counting.

The Desertion: When child support summons began arriving via Astral projection — interrupting actual combat missions — Zar'veth made a pragmatic decision. He stole from the warband's treasury, forged papers identifying himself as "Gary Dicksworth III, Human Merchant of Waterdeep," and stepped through a planar rift. He has been outrunning his obligations ever since.

The Alias: He chose "Gary" because it is the least threatening name in any language. "Dicksworth" because it sounds wealthy. "III" because it implies a family legacy that pays its debts. None of this has worked. He is nonetheless committed to the bit.

✦ Dungeon Master Arc Guide — For DM Eyes Only ✦
The Chad & Xal'ixx Arc
An Interleaved Story Guide
Two arcs. One collision course. Gary in the middle, as always.
6 Phases Interleaved Arcs Chad Approaches Xal'ixx Is The End
READ THIS FIRST — THE SHAPE OF THE STORY Chad and Xal'ixx are not separate arcs running in parallel. They are one story told from two directions. Chad is moving toward Gary because he chose to. Xal'ixx is moving toward Gary because she has to. Gary is standing still and calling it a plan. The comedy of the baby mama system has been the surface layer — underneath it, this is a story about a man who built something real by accident and has spent years pretending he didn't. These phases don't replace your campaign's main plot. They bleed into it. The DM's job is timing — knowing when to let a phase breathe and when to let it land.
Phase 1 — The Frequency (Early Campaign)
Chad has left home. He is traveling. He is 10 years old and his reality-bending powers are tied directly to his emotional state. As he moves through the world — excited, nervous, determined — the fabric of probability around him stutters. Gary feels it. He does not know what it is. The Moradin Situation journal entries begin.
  • ∿
    Gary's Surges Get Stranger Surge results start producing anomalies that don't fit the standard table — a single small boot appears, something smells faintly of smoke and scales, a goat materializes and stares at Gary with unusual intentionality. Gary updates his journal. His theory about Moradin becomes more elaborate and more wrong with each entry. The party notices the surges are changing. Gary insists it's fine.
  • ~
    Rumors — Towns With Impossible Weather NPCs mention in passing: a village three days east had a week where all the doors opened to the wrong rooms. A merchant reports his wagon arrived before it left. A shepherd says the probability of rain was 0% and it rained for six hours. These reports follow a route. The party may notice. Gary does not connect it.
  • ~
    The Locket Gets Warm Once per session, Gary's locket — Chad's portrait at age 7 — is slightly warm to the touch. Not hot. Just warm. Gary has no explanation for this. He checks it when he thinks no one is looking. He has checked it every day since the surges started changing. Do not draw attention to this mechanically. Just describe it if Gary reaches for it. Let it sit.
Phase 2 — The Party Finds Out (Mid-Early Campaign)
Gary talks in his sleep. He has always talked in his sleep. The party has never mentioned it because it's usually incoherent. But somewhere in the last few weeks, a name started appearing. Just one name. Repeated. Sometimes with instructions attached. "Don't take the coastal road, Chad." "Chad, the fire goes out before you sleep, not after." "Chad."
  • !
    The Discovery — Run This Out of Session Tell the other players privately — not Gary's player — that their characters have been hearing the name Chad for weeks. They don't know who Chad is yet. They know it matters. The group has silently agreed, without discussion, to wait and let Gary get there himself. This is the most important instruction in this entire document: the party knows. Gary doesn't know they know. No one says anything. This runs quietly in the background until the meeting.
  • ~
    How the Party Plays It Subtly. A party member might cover Gary with a blanket without explanation. Someone might steer conversation away from children unprompted. Someone might pause, almost say something, and then not. Gary's player should feel a vague warmth from the group without understanding why. This is the party quietly loving Gary in the only way they can — by giving him the space to arrive at it himself.
Phase 3 — Gary's "No Attachments" Monologue (The Session Before)
This phase is a single scene. It should happen naturally — ideally triggered by a moment in the main plot where the party is asked what they're fighting for, or what they'd lose if the mission failed. Gary gives his answer. It's the speech he always gives. The party listens with faces they have carefully arranged to reveal nothing.
Gary's Monologue — What He Says
"I'll tell you what I fight for. I fight for the next door. The next city. The next job that pays enough to stay ahead of — whatever's behind me. I don't fight for people because people are — they're complicated. They want things from you. They remember things you did. They show up. I travel light. Always have. No attachments. No — obligations. Nothing that slows you down or finds you in the middle of something important and — and I'm not — I don't have — there's no one. That's the whole point. There's no one."
DM note: Let the silence hold for a beat after he finishes. The party says nothing. One of them is looking at the door. Gary notices, but doesn't understand why. Then: the door opens.
Phase 4 — First Contact (The Meeting)
Chad walks in mid-sentence. He has found them — found Gary specifically — through a combination of his mother's tracking instincts, his own terrifying intelligence, and the fact that Gary's wild magic surges have been leaving a trail a child could follow. He is 10 years old. He is wearing traveling clothes that have been repaired several times. His eyes are Gary's eyes. His scales are barely visible — he is calm. He has been preparing for this moment.
  • ★
    The Entrance — Script This Beat Exactly The door opens. Chad steps in. He looks at Gary — really looks, the way someone looks at a thing they've been imagining for years and are now calibrating against reality. Then he looks at the party. He reads the room in approximately two seconds. He says: "He does this a lot, doesn't he." It is not a question. He sits down.
  • ~
    What Chad Does Not Do He does not cry. He does not accuse. He does not explain how he found Gary or how long it took or what it cost him. He does not ask for anything. He sits down, folds his hands on the table, and waits — with the absolute patience of someone who has been waiting his entire life and has decided that this particular wait is over. He is 10. This is more unsettling than anything else he could have done.
  • ∿
    The Reality Bleed The moment Chad sits down, Gary's wild magic threshold hits its maximum — surges on 1–5 for the rest of the session. More immediately: the locket in Gary's pocket is suddenly, uncomfortably hot. The candles in the room flicker without wind. A cup slides two inches to the left on its own. Chad glances at it. Looks back at Gary. Does not comment. He is used to this. Gary is not used to any of this.
  • !
    Gary's Move — The Only Thing That Matters Here Gary's player gets to decide what Gary does next. Do not rush this. Do not fill the silence. The party already knows. Chad already knows. The only person in the room who hasn't caught up is Gary. Whatever he says or does — whether he runs the rationalization ("business partner, high potential, seed investment") or whether something in him finally breaks toward something honest — that decision defines the rest of the campaign. Either is valid. Either is interesting. Let it breathe.
If Gary Runs The Rationalization
Chad listens to the whole thing with the patience of someone who expected exactly this. When Gary finishes, Chad reaches into his pack and puts the locket — Gary's locket, the one Gary left outside his village — on the table next to the one Gary is currently carrying. Two lockets. Same portrait. Different ages. He says: "You left that one when I was seven. I found the second one when I was nine. You were getting closer." Then he waits again.
If Gary Breaks Toward Something Honest
Chad's scales — barely visible until now — fade completely. Just for a moment. He looks like a kid. He says nothing. He nods once, slowly, the way someone does when a thing they'd stopped letting themselves hope for turns out to be real. The candles stop flickering. The cup slides back. Gary's locket goes cold. The party finds somewhere else to look.
Phase 5 — Chad In The Party (Post-Meeting)
Chad is not going home. He has decided. His reality-bending powers make him simultaneously the most useful and most unpredictable addition to any group dynamic. Gary officially refers to him as "a temporary operational asset." The party refers to him as Chad. Chad refers to Gary as "my associate." This is the funniest and most devastating thing he could possibly do.
  • ~
    Chad's Role In The Party Chad is a 10-year-old NPC with the social intelligence of someone three times his age and the impulse control of someone half his age. He is conniving, observant, and deeply funny in ways Gary recognizes and refuses to acknowledge. He gravitates toward whoever in the party treats him most like a person rather than a problem. He never asks Gary for anything directly. He just stays close. He has always been good at staying close to things that might leave.
  • ∿
    Reality Bleed — Living With Chad Small reality anomalies become background texture when Chad is present. Probability skews slightly in the party's favor when Chad is calm — dice feel lucky, the right door is always unlocked, the right person is always around the corner. When Chad is upset or scared, the opposite: things fail at wrong rates, distances stretch, the DM may call for saves on actions that wouldn't normally require them. The party learns to read his scales. Gary learns fastest, without admitting he's been watching.
  • ~
    The Slow Thing That Happens To Gary Gary stops running the "business partner" rationalization out loud. He still uses it internally. But the party stops hearing it. Instead Gary starts doing things — small, deniable things. Positioning himself between Chad and danger. Knowing Chad's preferences without being told. Leaving better gifts, less anonymously. He does not acknowledge any of this. If called out, he will explain it in operational terms. The explanation will not convince anyone, including Gary.
Phase 6 — Xal'ixx the Molten
· The Final Reckoning · Not A Comedy Encounter · Not A Team-Up · The End ·
WHEN SHE ARRIVES Late campaign. The main story has resolved. The party is in a genuinely good place — a victory, a celebration, a rare moment of optimism. Gary is present and, against all odds, seems to be present in the other sense too. Not running. Not rationalizing. Just — there. Chad is there. The party is there. For one brief window, everything is actually fine. Then the temperature rises. The sky outside changes color. Something ancient lands outside with a sound like the end of an argument.
THE STAGING — RUN THIS EXACTLY The door doesn't open dramatically. The wall ceases to be a wall. Xal'ixx does not fit through doors. She is enormous and ancient and her scales are the color of something that has been on fire for a thousand years. She looks at the room. She looks at Chad — a look that contains an entire conversation about leaving home without telling your mother, which she will be having with him later, at length. Then she looks at Gary. The room is very quiet. She says: "You stayed." It is not a compliment. It is not an accusation. It is the most dangerous thing she could say because it is simply true, and she sounds, underneath all of it, almost surprised.
WHAT XAL'IXX WANTS FROM GARY — THE REAL VERSION Not the money. She stopped caring about the money approximately the moment Chad packed his bag and walked out the door to find the man she raised him to know better than. She wants Gary to understand what he made. Not the chaos, not the wild magic, not the reality-bending — she has been watching Gary's child outthink warlords and connive his way through situations that would break adults, and she wants Gary to look at that and understand it didn't come from her discipline. It came from whatever Gary is underneath all the running. She hates that she knows this. She is here because someone has to say it and she has decided, furiously, that it will be her.
THE DEBT — WHAT SHE ACTUALLY SAYS After a long silence in which Gary does not run — which she clocks immediately — Xal'ixx says the following, or words to this effect: "I did not come here for gold, Gary. I came here because my son crossed three planes alone at ten years old to find a man who sends anonymous gifts and calls it investment, and when he walked through that door I am told you did not run. I want to know if that is permanent or if I am wasting my time." Then she waits. She is a dragon. She can wait longer than Gary has been alive.
Gary's Answer — The Only Moment That Matters In The Whole Campaign
There is no scripted answer. Gary's player decides. Whatever they say — whatever version of Gary has emerged across all the sessions, all the baby mama chaos, all the journal entries about Moradin, all the small deniable things — that answer is the campaign's ending note. The DM's job is to make sure Xal'ixx receives it with exactly as much grace as it deserves. No more. No less. She is not easy. She was never going to be easy. But she did fly across planes to ask the question in person, which means somewhere underneath the fire and the fury, she already knows what she's hoping to hear.
Chad During This Scene
Chad watches the whole exchange without speaking. His scales are fully visible — he is not calm, he is intensely, completely awake. He does not intervene. He has been waiting for this conversation longer than he has been waiting for Gary. When it ends — however it ends — he looks at Gary the same way he looked at him when he first walked through the door: calibrating. Updating the model. He has always been doing this. He will keep doing it. That's the whole point of having a business partner. You have to know what they're made of.
