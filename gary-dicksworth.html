Appearance
Tall and imposing. Yellow-green skin, angular features, eyes that sit slightly wrong in the face — unsettling in the specific way of something that learned to smile from observation rather than instinct. Carries himself like he owns every room. Half the time it works.
How He Acts
Always working an angle. Always three sentences into a pitch before you realize you're in one. Treats every interaction like a negotiation he intends to win — and usually does, right up until the moment he absolutely doesn't.
One Thing He Wants
Dicksworth & Co to become real. One slot at Co, always open, never filled. He hasn't examined what that actually means. He thinks about it constantly.
One Thing He Fears
Cows. Specifically, any cow he encounters in the wild may be a former mate he is desperately trying to forget. He calls this a "strategic wariness." He will not elaborate. He has elaborated to no one. He thinks about it every time he sees a cow, which is more often than anyone should.

📜 The Dossier of Gary Dicksworth III
Born: Zar'Veth the Evader, formerly of the 4th Astral Warband. Trained from birth to hunt Mind Flayers. Exceptional warrior. Catastrophically charming.

The Incident: Over fourteen years, Zar'Veth accumulated entanglements across multiple planes — three human women, two elves, a dwarf cleric (who has since involved her deity), a sea hag (he does not discuss this), a tiefling merchant, a half-orc paladin, and — in what his commanding officer described as "a diplomatic incident of staggering stupidity" — Xal'ixx the Molten, Red Dragon. Offspring: eight to eleven. Gary has stopped counting.

The Desertion: When child support summons began arriving via Astral projection — interrupting actual combat missions — Zar'Veth made a pragmatic decision. He stole from the warband's treasury, forged papers identifying himself as "Gary Dicksworth III, Human Merchant of Waterdeep," and stepped through a planar rift. He has been outrunning his obligations ever since.

The Alias: He chose "Gary" because it is the least threatening name in any language. "Dicksworth" because it sounds wealthy. "III" because it implies a family legacy that pays its debts. None of this has worked. He is nonetheless committed to the bit.

⚡ Personality
Aggressively confident. Interprets every magical catastrophe as "going according to plan." Has a practiced speech for every situation involving payment. Will attempt to negotiate his way out of anything, including a dragon.
· Ideal
Freedom. No court, dragon, or Astral Tribunal gets to tell Gary Dicksworth III what he owes. The cosmos belongs to those bold enough to take it and fast enough to run.
🔗 Bond — "Business Interest"
Gary does not love Chad. Gary has identified Chad as a future Co operative and has made several seed investments in his direction. The locket is due diligence. "Dicksworth & Co" is a contingency plan. There is only one slot at Co. Gary has not examined why.
💀 Flaw
Constitutionally incapable of admitting he cares about anything. Also: Xal'ixx the Molten is hunting him across planes, she is not letting this go, and she is the mother of a child who is currently traveling toward Gary unsupervised.

🎭 Roleplay Cheat Sheet
WHY ADVENTURING?
The bounty hunters. Plural. Also the dragon. Staying mobile, staying paid by others, staying alive and ahead of nine active summons.
WHY WORK WITH A GROUP?
A party is excellent cover. Gary cannot currently afford to operate alone. He frames this as "enjoying the camaraderie." The party has not pushed back on this framing.
GENERAL PERSONALITY
Smooth talker. Always working an angle. Warm, funny, impossible to fully distrust — which is exactly what he wants. Every conversation is a negotiation he intends to win.
REACTION TO DANGER
Negotiate first. Misty Step second. If cornered: unhinged magic violence, delivered with complete confidence that this was the plan all along.
WHAT MOTIVATES HIM
Survival, freedom, and making Dicksworth & Co real — though he hasn't examined what that means or why there's only one slot at Co.
⚠ DO NOT APPROACH COWS
Any cow encountered in the wild may be a former mate Gary is desperately trying to forget. He calls this strategic wariness. He will not discuss it further.

Two triggers. When either fires — go to the Baby Mamas tab, pick the next pursuer in rotation, and roll her d6 for the relevant trigger column.
⚔ Trigger A — Gary cornered / no escape
Roll her Trigger A d6. Almost always creates an exit for Gary — the chaos reshuffles the situation in the party's favor.
⚔ Trigger B — Critical social moment
Roll her Trigger B d6. Results range from catastrophic to miraculous — each baby mama's personality shapes the outcome differently.

1Gary cornered or in critical social moment
2Go to Baby Mamas tab → pick next pursuer in rotation
3Read her entrance line aloud
4Roll her d6 for the matching trigger column
5Gary gets one free Misty Step or Deception at advantage
6She keeps chasing. Original threat may now chase both of them.
⚔ Key Rules
⚔ Baby mama encounters max once every 2–3 sessions — frequency kills the joke
⚔ Each baby mama gets 1 appearance then retires. Mark her slot. Move on.
⚔ Roll the Tactical Resource every scene — this is non-negotiable. It defines Gary's mood, capability, and chaos level for that scene.
⚔ Gary's character moments are flavor, not plot. The baby mamas, the flask, the locket — these enrich scenes. They don't drive the campaign. Let the party's story lead.
⚔ Chad shows up once, saves the party, says nothing, leaves. See Chad tab. That's the whole bit.
⚔ New player table. Keep it fun, keep it moving. If a mechanic is slowing the game down, skip it. The goal is a good session, not perfect execution of the system.

01 · Thrak'veth Ironbone
Human Warrior · Methodical · Has done this before
○
"Gary Dicksworth. I've been looking forward to this for fourteen months. I brought the paperwork."
"Don't. I've already mapped your exits. All four of them."
"Gentlemen — apologies. This won't take long, and then he's all yours."
🎲 Trigger A — Combat / Cornered (d6)
1Briefs the enemies on Gary's stats before stepping back. They gain advantage against him this round. She takes notes on the outcome.
2Blocks Gary's exit with her body and a clipboard. Enemies get a free attack on him while he's distracted trying to get past her.
3Positions herself between Gary and the biggest threat. Neither can move past her without a contested check. Combat stalls one round.
4Begins reading the debt aloud mid-combat. Both sides lose focus. Everyone delays their next action one round including Gary.
5Decides a dead Gary voids the contract. Removes the nearest threat efficiently. Gary is alive. She is now his biggest problem in the room.
6Has already spoken to the enemies. They stand down. Gary has no idea what she promised them on his behalf. Neither does the party. Thrak'veth smiles for the first time.
🎲 Trigger B — Social / Critical Moment (d6)
1Produces documentation. To the noble. In detail. The meeting ends.
2Knows the noble professionally. She is credible here. Gary is immediately not.
3Hands the noble her card mid-negotiation. Noble pockets it. Gary must make DC 12 Persuasion to reclaim the room's attention.
4Accidentally confirms something Gary just claimed. Covers her mouth. Too late. Deal stays alive on a technicality.
5Corroborates Gary's story just enough for the deal to hold — then announces he has outstanding obligations. Noble is now curious. Gary must decide how curious.
6Helps Gary close. She wants him successful and indebted, in that order. Deal closes. Everyone in the room just watched her make sure of it.
⚔ Wants the money. Will settle for Gary signing something in front of witnesses.
02 · Sylmara Dawnweave
Elven Ranger · Glacially calm · Already three steps ahead
○
"I've been in this city for three days. You didn't notice."
"I have an arrow nocked. I'd prefer not to use it — the paperwork is worse than Thrak'veth's."
"I'll wait. Unlike your obligations, I have centuries."
🎲 Trigger A — Combat / Cornered (d6)
1Calls out Gary's position, AC, and movement speed to the room. Factually. As a courtesy. Enemies gain advantage this round.
2Appears on the enemies' side of the room. She is not with them. She is here for Gary. The distinction is currently academic.
3Shoots the lead enemy's weapon out of their hand. Combat pauses. Gary has one free action before everyone recalibrates.
4Fires a warning shot between Gary and his nearest exit. Neither Gary nor the enemy can move toward it without risking another. One round stalemate.
5Clears two enemies with two arrows and zero expression. Fight gets easier. She is now the only thing between Gary and the door.
6The enemies were already going to stand down. She arranged this before Gary arrived. She lets him figure this out on his own timeline.
🎲 Trigger B — Social / Critical Moment (d6)
1Has been in the building three days and knows something damaging about Gary. Uses it. On Gary. In front of the noble.
2Sits uninvited beside the noble. Introduces herself as someone familiar with Gary's history. Says nothing further. Noble starts asking questions.
3Tells the noble to halve whatever Gary just claimed. Noble is skeptical. DC 13 Persuasion to recover or the deal stalls.
4Mentions she has tracked Gary for forty years. Noble finds this impressive. Gary gets advantage on his next roll and cannot explain why.
5Stands near Gary saying nothing. Noble asks who she is. Gary says colleague. She neither confirms nor denies it. The ambiguity somehow helps him close.
6Provides an alibi that is accurate and misleading in equal measure. Noble is satisfied. Sylmara tells Gary quietly what she expects in return. He doesn't agree. She notes that he didn't disagree. She has centuries.
⚔ Wants Gary to say out loud that he left. Not got lost. Left. She has centuries to wait.
03 · Lady Bryndis Stonehammer
Dwarf Cleric · Righteous · Moradin is genuinely invested in this
○
"The light of Moradin led me here, Gary. A god. Personally involved."
"I'd like to introduce you to a Writ of Divine Debt. I had it invented. For you specifically."
"You may continue your foolishness — I've waited twelve years. What's five more minutes."
🎲 Trigger A — Combat / Cornered (d6)
1Casts Sacred Flame at Gary. DC 13 Dex or 1d8 radiant damage. She considers this a warning.
2Joins the fight on the wrong side. She is fighting everyone. She is also praying. The two activities are not in conflict for her.
3Attempts to excommunicate Gary from a religion he doesn't practice. Theologically meaningless. Every combatant stops for one full round trying to process it.
4Casts Bless on the enemies for fairness. Then immediately questions this aloud. Both sides lose a turn watching her argue with herself.
5Casts Sanctuary on Gary by accident. He cannot be attacked. He also cannot attack. She is furious. He is protected and furious in equal measure.
6Moradin apparently intervenes. Light, enemies fleeing, Gary unharmed. Bryndis is on her knees. Gary is standing in the middle of it looking like he planned this. He absolutely did not.
🎲 Trigger B — Social / Critical Moment (d6)
1Already spoke to the noble's priest. The church has a different account of Gary. The noble trusts the church.
2Produces the Writ of Divine Debt. It has a seal. The noble is a religious man. He is reading it slowly.
3Blesses the meeting without being asked. Noble is touched. Gary must sit through it without reacting. DC 10 Composure or the noble notices his face.
4Tells the noble Gary is on a path. Noble softens. Gary gets one free Persuasion reroll this scene but must let Bryndis finish talking first.
5She and Gary argue theology for three minutes. Noble agrees to Gary's terms just to end it. Neither Gary nor Bryndis considers this a win.
6Prays for Gary's soul mid-negotiation with genuine feeling. Noble is moved. Deal closes in the silence that follows. Bryndis looks at Gary. Gary looks at the floor.
⚔ Divine justice. Gary is banned from the afterlife until this is resolved. Moradin's words, not hers.
04 · Vel "The Quiet" Ashborn
Tiefling Guild Matriarch · Never raises her voice · Already owns the room
○
"Gary. Don't make that face. I'm not here to cause a scene. I am the scene."
"I've already spoken to your friends. Very productive conversation about your financial history."
"You can run. I've already arranged what happens after. Would you like to know how it ends?"
🎲 Trigger A — Combat / Cornered (d6)
1Already paid the enemies before Gary arrived. They were never going to hurt him. She lets him figure this out after the first round. She found it useful information.
2Sits nearby and watches. Gary fights knowing she is watching and has already arranged something. Disadvantage on all actions this round.
3Passes Gary one piece of information about the lead enemy. They back off immediately. Gary doesn't know what she knows. She doesn't explain.
4Tips off the city guard. Everyone is detained. She is not detained. She is never detained. Gary loses the scene completely. She visits him later.
5Whispers something to the lead enemy. They stand down without explanation. Gary is safe. No one will ever know what she said including the party.
6The enemies work for her. She mentions this after a full round of combat. Gary has just spent sixty seconds fighting her employees. She calls it a demonstration.
🎲 Trigger B — Social / Critical Moment (d6)
1Owns the building. Noble rents from her. The power dynamic in the room shifts the moment she walks in and everyone feels it.
2Sits beside the noble instead of Gary. Starts her own conversation. Gary watches his deal become Vel's deal in real time.
3Vouches for Gary. Adds "within reason." Nobody knows what that means. The noble is reassured anyway.
4Slides Gary a note with exactly what to say. It works. He doesn't know what he agreed to by using it. She does.
5Noble asks who she is. She says former associate. It lands well. Gary closes the deal and spends the rest of the scene wondering what that meant.
6Already negotiated this deal before Gary arrived. Let him run the whole meeting. His version got him less than hers would have. She doesn't say how much less. She doesn't have to.
⚔ Unknown. She has never said. This is the most unsettling thing about her.
05 · Mira of the Tides
Half-Orc Paladin · Believes Gary can be redeemed · She is wrong
○
"Gary! I'm not angry. I'm concerned. There's a difference and I need you to understand that right now."
"Running is just evidence of emotional unavailability and we can work through that — Gary. Gary please stop walking while I'm—"
"I've spoken to several of your children. They miss you. I told them you were on a journey. Are you on a journey, Gary?"
🎲 Trigger A — Combat / Cornered (d6)
1Tries to de-escalate through dialogue with the enemies. They pause one round. She has now given them Gary's full backstory. They are no longer confused. They are motivated.
2Heals Gary without asking. He didn't need healing. He needed an exit. He is at full HP with nowhere to go.
3Compelled Duels the biggest threat so Gary can handle the rest. Narrates her belief in him throughout. He cannot make her stop. It is very loud.
4Smites an enemy with full conviction, drawing every eye in the room. Gary has one completely free action. She considers this a coaching moment.
5Shouts specific encouragement mid-combat referencing things Gary would rather the party not know. Gary rolls his next check at advantage. The party heard everything.
6Delivers a letter mid-combat. Gary rolls everything at advantage for one round. Mira reads excerpts aloud to the party before Gary can stop her. He moves faster. She matches his pace.
🎲 Trigger B — Social / Critical Moment (d6)
1Tells the noble about Gary's accountability journey. With specific examples. Noble is now concerned about Gary on a human level before the deal is even discussed.
2Asks to pray for the meeting. Noble agrees. She prays for Gary specifically and at length. Gary cannot leave without being rude. The noble is watching his face.
3Asks Gary in front of the noble how he has been feeling lately. DC 12 Charisma save or Gary visibly reacts. Noble notices regardless.
4Accidentally says exactly what Gary needed the noble to hear. Deal advances. Noble now expects Gary to be the person Mira just described. He is not that person.
5Puts her hand on Gary's shoulder and tells the noble he's trying. Noble softens. Deal closes. Gary feels something he doesn't examine.
6Speech so compelling the noble offers a better deal. Mira follows Gary out still talking. The party witnesses all of it. Gary walks faster. She has more to say.
⚔ Gary's redemption. She will never stop believing in him. This is the most dangerous thing about her.
🐉 Chad — The One Appearance
Chad is not an arc. He shows up once, when the party is genuinely losing — not struggling, actually losing. Roll d6 on arrival for what impossible thing just happened.
ONE RULE ACROSS ALL RESULTS Chad never speaks during the encounter. On a 6 he looks at the biggest threat with an expression that apparently communicates something. At the end, he looks at Gary for exactly two seconds. Leaves. Gary's player decides if Gary says anything. The party gets no explanation. That's the whole bit.
🎲 The Chad Roll — d6 on arrival
1 A door appears in a wall that had no door. It leads somewhere useful. Chad is standing next to it looking bored. He didn't knock it open. It just exists now. He shrugs.
2 The lead enemy trips. Spectacularly. At the exact moment they were about to end someone. No visible cause. Chad is across the room eating something. He looks up. Makes eye contact with Gary. Goes back to eating.
3 Every light source goes out simultaneously. When they come back on three seconds later, one enemy is unconscious and Chad is standing exactly where he was before. He has not moved. Allegedly.
4 Gary's wild magic surge fires — but the result is whatever the party needed most in that moment. Not random. Precisely correct. Chad is nearby. Gary's locket is warm. Nobody comments on this.
5 Time stutters. Everyone in the room experiences half a second twice. In that half second, the tactical situation changes in the party's favor. Chad blinks slowly. Like a cat.
6 Chad looks at the biggest threat in the room. The biggest threat sits down. Not stunned, not charmed — just done. Chad looks at Gary for exactly two seconds. Leaves. The threat stays sitting. Nobody knows what was said. Nothing was said.
